# Interest Profile

This document defines what matters to me for AI-powered relevance scoring and digest generation.
Edit this to tune what the system surfaces vs. buries.

## High Relevance (always surface these)

- **AI/ML applied to enterprise software** — especially people analytics, HR tech, workforce planning.
  I work in this space (ZeroedIn People Analytics) and need to know what competitors and the broader
  market are doing. Anything about AI in B2B SaaS, enterprise AI adoption, or AI features in analytics
  platforms is directly relevant.
- **AWS infrastructure** — ECS, Bedrock, Lambda, Aurora, and related services. Architecture patterns,
  cost optimization, new service announcements.
- **Self-hosted / homelab tooling** — Proxmox, Docker, LXC, networking (Headscale/Tailscale),
  monitoring (Grafana), media servers, NAS, anything that makes a homelab better.
- **AI policy, safety, and industry dynamics** — regulation, model releases, capability benchmarks,
  lab drama, open-source model developments. I want the signal, not the hype.
- **Deep analytical essays** — writers like Zvi Mowshowitz, Adam Mastrionni, Scott Alexander, etc.
  Even when the topic seems tangential, the analysis itself is valuable. Always flag these as worth reading.
- **Photography software and AI editing tools** — Lightroom, Capture One, AI-powered photo editing,
  computational photography. I run a YouTube channel covering this.

## Medium Relevance (include in digest if interesting, don't bury)

- **General tech industry news** — major acquisitions, layoffs, product launches from big players.
  Only if genuinely significant, not routine press releases.
- **Game design and indie dev** — Godot engine, game mechanics theory, notable indie releases.
- **MTG Commander format** — new set releases, notable card evaluations, meta shifts, budget deck ideas.
  Not standard/modern format unless it crosses over.
- **TTRPGs** — D&D, OSR, Mythic Bastionland, Delta Green, system design discussions.
  Not actual play recaps or adventure module reviews unless they're notable.
- **Linux desktop / Wayland / Arch ecosystem** — notable developments, not routine package updates.
- **Parenting / new parent stuff** — only if it's genuinely useful practical advice, not engagement bait.

## Low Relevance (skip unless truly exceptional)

- Cryptocurrency / web3 / blockchain
- Mainstream consumer tech reviews (phone reviews, laptop roundups)
- Marketing, growth hacking, SEO, "how to grow your newsletter" content
- Celebrity news, entertainment industry gossip
- Sports
- Routine product update announcements / changelogs
- Political horse-race coverage (policy substance is fine, campaign strategy is not)

## Digest Formatting Preferences

- Lead with the 3-5 most relevant items, each with a 1-2 sentence summary explaining
  WHY it's relevant to me specifically, not just what it says.
- Group remaining items by theme, not by source.
- For dense analytical posts (Zvi, etc.), note the key thesis and whether it connects
  to anything I care about. Don't try to summarize the whole thing — just tell me if
  I should read it and why.
- Include direct links to the FreshRSS article view so I can click through.
- Keep it scannable. I want to spend 5 minutes on this email, max.
